Valley Of Flowers National Park
The Valley of Flowers, located in the Nanda Devi Biosphere Reserve’s second core zone, is a stunning natural wonder in the Chamoli district of Uttaranchal.
It was first discovered by British mountaineers Frank S. Smythe and R.L. Holdsworth in 1931. Frank S. Smythe later revisited the valley in 1937 and published a book titled “The Valley Of Flowers,” which gained international recognition.
Spanning an area of 87.5 sq. km. at an altitude ranging from 3250 to 6750 meters, the valley remains covered in snow from November to May.
However, in June, when the snow melts, the valley undergoes a breathtaking transformation with the blooming of around 300 species of wildflowers.
The floral composition changes constantly, offering a vibrant display of colours throughout the three-month blooming period.
The Valley of Flowers is also home to diverse wildlife and serves as a habitat for numerous bird species.
Enclosed by majestic peaks such as Gauri Parbat, Rataban, Kuntkhal, Saptsring, and Nilgiri Parbat, the valley became a designated National Park in 1982 to protect its pristine beauty and ecological balance.
Hemkund Sahib Gurdwara
Hemkund Sahib is a holy place near the Valley of Flowers, situated at an elevation of 4329 meters. The main attraction here is the Hemkund Lokpal Sahib Lake, which offers a stunning view of a clear lake surrounded by seven snow-covered peaks of the Himalayas.
According to Sikh beliefs, Guru Govind Singh, the tenth Guru, meditated by this lake in a previous life. People from different religions visit this place, including Sikhs, Hindus, and others, seeking spiritual solace.
Hemkund Sahib is also associated with the story of Lakshman, the brother of Lord Rama. It is believed that Lakshman meditated at this spot and found healing after being injured in a battle. There is a temple dedicated to Lakshman by the lakeside.
With its serene lake and breathtaking views, this holy place attracts devotees and nature lovers who come to experience the tranquillity and beauty amidst the mighty Himalayas.
